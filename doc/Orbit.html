Simple Orbit

  • Pick a circular orbit centered around a Mars-like planet
  • Kepler's Third Law for a circular orbit:
T=2πR3GM \begin{aligned} T &= 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{R^3}{GM}} \\ \end{aligned} T​=2πGMR3​​​

where:

  • TTT is the orbital period
  • RRR is the radius of the circular orbit
  • GGG is the gravitational constant
  • MMM is the mass of the planet
  • For relatively low altitude (160km):
T=2πR3GM≐2π(3.39×106+1.6×105)36.67×10−11×6.39×1023≐107 minutes \begin{aligned} T &= 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{R^3}{GM}} \\ &\doteq 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{(3.39\times10^6+1.6\times10^5)^3} {6.67\times10^{-11}\times6.39\times10^{23}}} \\ &\doteq 107\text{ minutes} \\ \end{aligned} T​=2πGMR3​​≐2π6.67×10−11×6.39×1023(3.39×106+1.6×105)3​​≐107 minutes​
